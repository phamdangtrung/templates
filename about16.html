📝 my story

Welcome to my corner of the web! I'm a digital enthusiast who fell in love with the aesthetics of the late 90s and early 2000s — an era when the internet felt like a frontier of endless possibilities.

Back then, websites were personal. They were expressions of identity, creativity, and optimism. The Y2K aesthetic captured that spirit perfectly — glossy interfaces, chrome accents, floating bubbles, and the belief that technology would make everything better.

This blog is my tribute to that era. Here, I explore the intersection of design, technology, and nostalgia. I experiment with CSS, share thoughts on web culture, and celebrate the digital dreams of the millennium.

When I'm not coding or writing, you can find me collecting retro tech, exploring abandoned websites, or trying to convince people that Comic Sans isn't all bad.
